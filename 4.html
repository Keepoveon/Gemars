SMD stands for "Surface Mount Device". It is a type of electronic component that is designed to be mounted directly onto the surface of a printed circuit board (PCB). Unlike traditional through-hole components that have leads that pass through holes in the PCB, SMD components have small metal tabs or pads that are soldered directly to the surface of the PCB.
SMD components are typically smaller than through-hole components, which allows for more components to be placed on a single PCB and can help reduce the overall size of electronic devices. SMD components are also often faster and more reliable than through-hole components due to their smaller size and shorter connection paths.

Ohm's Law is a fundamental principle in electronics that describes the relationship between current, voltage, and resistance in an electrical circuit. It states that the current passing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points, and inversely proportional to the resistance between them.
